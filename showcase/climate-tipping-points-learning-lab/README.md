# Climate Tipping Points Learning Lab

## What This Demonstrates

The **Climate Tipping Points Learning Lab** is a flagship orchestration demo for the Education Agent Skills Library. It shows how multiple evidence-based education skills can combine into a coherent learning system: curriculum design, systems thinking, AI tutoring, student-facing learning safeguards, AI literacy, inclusive design, assessment validity, and implementation analytics.

The demo is intentionally advanced. Climate tipping points are scientifically complex, emotionally loaded, politically contested, and vulnerable to shallow AI explanations. That makes the topic an excellent stress test for the library. A weak education agent would produce a neat unit plan. This showcase produces a learning architecture that protects cognitive work, checks transfer, exposes AI hallucination risk, and gives teachers evidence about what students can do independently.

## Start Here

Open [index.html](./index.html) in a browser. That is the actual teacher-facing demo app.

The Markdown files are the source notes behind the app. They are useful for inspection, editing, and curriculum review, but the app is the thing to show people.

## Audience

- **Learners:** Year 12, IB Diploma, AP Environmental Science / Human Geography, A-level Geography or Biology, or first-year undergraduate general education
- **Educators:** science, geography, environmental systems, global politics, interdisciplinary studies
- **Reviewers:** professional investors, technical teams, curriculum developers, school founders, innovation leads

## Core Promise

Students do not simply learn about climate tipping points. They learn to:

- explain nonlinear feedback systems with evidence
- distinguish genuine understanding from assisted fluency
- audit AI-generated scientific claims
- map systems without collapsing into blame or despair
- design bounded, responsible local action
- prove independent transfer after scaffolded AI support

## Demo Flow

The strongest live demonstration takes 18-22 minutes:

1. **Investor/founder opening:** show the executive brief and explain the market problem: current AI tutors often create fluent dependency rather than durable learning.
2. **Curriculum proof:** show the unit blueprint and assessment model.
3. **Technical proof:** show the orchestration map, knowledge component model, and evidence tags.
4. **Learning proof:** run the live student transcript: retrieve-first, hint ladder, teach-back, transfer, unassisted checkpoint.
5. **Trust proof:** run the hallucination hunt and show how students verify AI claims.
6. **Equity proof:** show the inclusive access layer and explain how the system keeps cognitive demand high.
7. **Product proof:** show the teacher dashboard specification and sample student artifacts.
8. **Governance proof:** show privacy and data boundaries.
9. **Quality proof:** show the excellence criteria and QA report.

## Why This Should Impress

This package demonstrates that the skills library can become a serious education infrastructure layer. It can power curriculum design, AI tutoring, formative assessment, learning analytics, inclusive adaptation, and AI literacy from one coherent pedagogical spine.

The point is not that the AI writes more educational content. The point is that the AI behaves like a learning system.
