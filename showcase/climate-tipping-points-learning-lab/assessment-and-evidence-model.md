# Assessment And Evidence Model

## Final Performance Task

Create and defend a **Climate Tipping Point Systems Brief** for a real audience.

The brief must:

1. explain one climate tipping system accurately
2. show feedback loops and possible thresholds
3. use credible evidence with uncertainty language
4. include an AI audit trail
5. propose bounded action using control, influence, concern, and adult/institutional responsibility
6. compare scaffolded AI learning evidence with unassisted performance

## Success Criteria

| Criterion | Excellent | Secure | Developing | Not Yet |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Systems explanation | Explains variables, causal links, feedback closure, threshold reasoning, and uncertainty with precision | Explains variables, links, and feedback with minor gaps | Describes causes but weak loop closure or threshold reasoning | Lists climate effects without systems logic |
| Evidence use | Uses credible sources, verifies claims, and distinguishes evidence from inference | Uses credible sources with mostly accurate claims | Uses sources but with weak verification or overclaiming | Relies on unsupported or AI-generated claims |
| AI literacy | Provides a clear audit trail showing source reconstruction and claim revision | Identifies and checks AI claims | Notes that AI may be wrong but verification is shallow | Trusts or rejects AI without method |
| Transfer | Applies feedback reasoning to a new climate or non-climate context | Applies reasoning to a similar climate context | Repeats taught example with limited adaptation | Cannot transfer beyond model answer |
| Bounded action | Proposes feasible action with control/influence/concern distinctions and unintended consequence checks | Proposes plausible action with some boundary awareness | Proposes action but with vague agency or feasibility | Uses heroic or blame-based action framing |
| Independent learning evidence | Shows strong unassisted checkpoint evidence and honest confidence calibration | Shows completed unassisted evidence with reflection | Provides mostly scaffolded evidence | No clear evidence of independent performance |

## Formative Evidence Stream

| Moment | Evidence Captured | Decision It Supports |
|---|---|---|
| Opening retrieval | confidence, recall quality, misconceptions | What to teach next |
| Feedback-loop tutor | knowledge component, hint level, error type | Which subskill needs practice |
| Teach-back | explanation gaps | Whether student can organise understanding |
| Transfer bridge | near/far transfer result | Whether learning is portable |
| AI audit | claim verification accuracy | Whether student can use AI responsibly |
| Unassisted checkpoint | independent performance | Whether scaffolded success became real capability |
| Weekly review | patterns across sessions | Student goal-setting and teacher intervention |

## Validity Checks

The assessment is valid only if:

- students are assessed on systems reasoning, not just presentation design
- AI audit evidence is judged by verification quality, not number of sources
- action proposals are judged by bounded responsibility, not moral intensity
- unassisted evidence is kept separate from scaffolded evidence
- uncertainty language is rewarded when scientifically appropriate

## Reliability Supports

- Use the same six criteria for all students.
- Require evidence annotations for each systems claim.
- Use short oral defences to confirm ownership of understanding.
- Moderate three sample briefs before final marking.
- Ask students to explain one unfamiliar feedback loop during the defence.

## Oral Defence Questions

1. Which variable in your system has the most leverage, and why?
2. Where does your feedback loop close?
3. What claim did AI make that you had to revise?
4. What is one uncertainty you should not hide?
5. What can students control here, and what belongs to adults or institutions?
6. What did you do independently that you could not do at the start?
