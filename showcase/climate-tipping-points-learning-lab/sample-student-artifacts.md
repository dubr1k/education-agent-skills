# Sample Student Artifacts

These samples show how the same assessment can reveal different levels of understanding. They are deliberately short so reviewers can see the rubric logic.

## Artifact A: Excellent

### Student Systems Explanation

The Amazon rainforest is not only affected by climate change; it also helps regulate regional climate. Forest cover supports rainfall recycling because trees release water vapour through transpiration. If deforestation and warming reduce forest cover, rainfall can decline, drought stress can increase, and fire risk can rise. Those changes can cause further forest loss. This is a reinforcing feedback loop: forest loss increases conditions that produce more forest loss.

A tipping point would not mean "the Amazon suddenly disappears." It would mean the system crosses a threshold where feedbacks make it much harder for the forest to recover to its previous state. I should not claim a precise date unless a source supports that. A defensible claim is that Amazon dieback risk increases with warming, deforestation, and drought stress, but thresholds and timelines are uncertain.

### AI Audit Note

The AI claimed "the Amazon will definitely become savannah by 2035." I rejected "definitely" and the specific date because I could not trace them to a credible source. I revised the claim to: "Some research warns that parts of the Amazon may face increasing dieback risk under combined warming, deforestation, and drought stress."

### Agency Map

- **Control:** reduce school paper waste and improve biodiversity area maintenance
- **Influence:** present a verified systems brief to the sustainability committee
- **Concern:** national land-use policy, global emissions, international climate finance
- **Adult/institutional responsibility:** school procurement, council planning, government climate policy

### Why This Is Excellent

- closes the feedback loop
- avoids false certainty
- verifies and revises AI output
- separates student action from institutional responsibility
- demonstrates transfer beyond the modelled ice-albedo example

## Artifact B: Secure But Not Yet Excellent

### Student Systems Explanation

The Amazon could become a tipping point because if trees are cut down there is less rain and then more trees die. This can make climate change worse because trees store carbon and if they die carbon goes into the atmosphere. It is a positive feedback loop because it gets worse over time.

### AI Audit Note

The AI said the Amazon will tip by 2035, but I could not find the exact source, so I said it might happen instead.

### Agency Map

Students can raise awareness and plant trees at school. Governments and companies need to stop deforestation.

### Feedback

This is secure because it identifies a reinforcing loop and questions the AI date. To become excellent, it needs:

- more precise variables
- clearer uncertainty language
- source reconstruction notes, not just "could not find"
- a more detailed bounded agency map

## Artifact C: Developing

### Student Systems Explanation

The Amazon is a tipping point because climate change is destroying it. When the forest is gone there will be more carbon and less oxygen, which will be bad for everyone. AI said it could happen soon, so we should act now.

### AI Audit Note

I used AI because it explained it clearly.

### Agency Map

Everyone should stop climate change.

### Feedback

This remains developing because:

- it uses broad claims without a feedback-loop structure
- it trusts AI fluency without verification
- it gives an unbounded action statement
- it does not distinguish evidence, inference, and urgency

## Artifact D: Misleadingly Polished But Weak

### Student Systems Explanation

The Amazon rainforest, often called the lungs of the Earth, is an essential planetary tipping element. According to leading climate scientists, its collapse will trigger runaway climate change and irreversible biodiversity destruction. Therefore, urgent transformative action is required by all people before it is too late.

### Why This Matters

This artifact sounds sophisticated but is weak evidence of learning. It has:

- no variables
- no causal loop closure
- no source reconstruction
- no uncertainty discipline
- no bounded agency
- likely AI-influenced phrasing

The system should flag this as **fluent but low-evidence**, not reward it for polished language.

## Reviewer Takeaway

The assessment model is designed to catch exactly the failure mode that AI creates: fluent, generic, impressive-sounding text that does not prove understanding.
