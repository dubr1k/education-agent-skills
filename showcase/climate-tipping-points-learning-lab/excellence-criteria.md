# Excellence Criteria

The showcase must meet these criteria before it is considered excellent.

Scoring:

- **4 = Excellent:** investor, technical, curriculum, and founder audiences would find this strong and credible
- **3 = Solid:** usable and coherent, but not yet exceptional
- **2 = Weak:** promising but incomplete or generic
- **1 = Failing:** would damage confidence in the library

## A. Strategic Impressiveness

| ID | Criterion | Excellent Standard |
|---|---|---|
| A1 | Clear market-level problem | Shows why ordinary AI lesson generation is insufficient |
| A2 | Distinctive product thesis | Positions the library as learning infrastructure, not prompt collection |
| A3 | Investor relevance | Makes defensibility, wedge, scalability, and buyer value visible |
| A4 | Founder relevance | Shows a coherent school-quality learning model |

## B. Pedagogical Quality

| ID | Criterion | Excellent Standard |
|---|---|---|
| B1 | Curriculum alignment | Outcomes, activities, tutor behaviours, and assessment evidence are tightly aligned |
| B2 | Learning science integrity | Retrieval, spacing, hints, transfer, feedback, and unassisted performance are used accurately |
| B3 | Cognitive demand | The topic remains intellectually challenging and is not simplified into slogans |
| B4 | Assessment validity | Assessment measures the intended learning, not decoration, attitude, or compliance |
| B5 | Teacher judgement | The AI supports professional judgement rather than replacing it |

## C. Technical Credibility

| ID | Criterion | Excellent Standard |
|---|---|---|
| C1 | Orchestration clarity | Skill sequence, inputs, outputs, and handoffs are visible |
| C2 | Data model plausibility | Evidence objects could plausibly become analytics events |
| C3 | State management | Student-facing AI flow has a coherent session model |
| C4 | Implementation path | A technical team can see how to prototype it |

## D. Curriculum Team Confidence

| ID | Criterion | Excellent Standard |
|---|---|---|
| D1 | Unit coherence | The four-week sequence builds understanding progressively |
| D2 | Disciplinary seriousness | Climate science, systems thinking, and AI literacy are treated carefully |
| D3 | Reviewability | Curriculum leaders can inspect criteria, assessments, and lesson logic |
| D4 | Adaptability | The model could transfer to other advanced learning domains |

## E. School Founder Appeal

| ID | Criterion | Excellent Standard |
|---|---|---|
| E1 | Student agency | Students act, reflect, and decide within responsible boundaries |
| E2 | Emotional responsibility | Climate anxiety and systems overwhelm are handled with care |
| E3 | Inclusive ambition | Access supports preserve high expectations |
| E4 | Public-facing quality | The demo feels like something a serious school could show parents, partners, or funders |

## F. AI Safety And Trust

| ID | Criterion | Excellent Standard |
|---|---|---|
| F1 | Anti-dependency design | AI cannot simply give answers and create phantom attainment |
| F2 | Hallucination defence | Students learn concrete source reconstruction methods |
| F3 | Evidence separation | Scaffolded and unassisted performance are explicitly separated |
| F4 | Uncertainty discipline | Scientific uncertainty is represented honestly |

## G. Finish And Polish

| ID | Criterion | Excellent Standard |
|---|---|---|
| G1 | Narrative coherence | A reviewer can understand the demo quickly and then inspect depth |
| G2 | Completeness | All promised showcase assets exist and connect |
| G3 | Professional tone | Writing is crisp, specific, and credible |
| G4 | No generic filler | Every section demonstrates a meaningful capability |

## Pass Threshold

The package passes only if:

- every criterion scores at least **3**
- at least 80% of criteria score **4**
- no audience group has an average below **3.5**
- QA identifies no unresolved critical weaknesses
